The Underground Shelter

People say our school under the ground is like a bomb shelter. So secluded that even the day’s weather eludes us until we step outside. They all say this as a negative thing. I see the shelter analogy differently. Bnos Yaakov really is a shelter. It’s a place where we retreat to prepare ourselves for the war above. In it, we train like soldiers every single day, because we are soldiers- soldiers of Hashem.
And there is a war , it’s called Galus. At Bnos Yaakov we learn how to fight and survive a world full of immorality and influence. When we do emerge -not at the end of the day, but after four years-we are ready. We are model soldiers. Campaigning, fighting, and aiding in the win of this three thousand year war. Our eventual victory will be all thanks to this little shelter under the ground!

Suri Moskowitz, 10th

Too Great for Words

It seems almost like an impossible task to try to name everything Bnos Yaakov has given me in the past two and a half years. The inspiring lessons from our wonderful principals and teachers, the sight of girls laughing and talking in the hallways each morning, and the feeling of unity as the school dances by chagiga…. It’s a feeling that is truly difficult to put on paper. The greatest things in life can only be understood and appreciated by actually experiencing them, and Bnos Yaakov is one of them.
One aspect of our school that stands out to me is the inspirational guest speakers we are privileged to hear quite often. One particular speaker that made an impact on my life is Rabbi Milstein, who came to speak at last year’s Shabbaton. He spoke about what it means to be happy with our lives by changing our view of the world. Rabbi Milstein told a story about a woman who was miserable with her life circumstances, to the point that she no longer wanted to continue living. Rabbi Milstein told us to imagine that the woman was blind, in addition to all the other problems that she was experiencing. What if she could get her sight back? Wouldn’t she be the happiest woman in the world? We can all be the happiest people, if we learn to appreciate all the gifts we have been given. When Rabbi Milstein spoke about this, it made me realize how many things there are in my life that I never took the time to think about. This is just one of the experiences in Bnos Yaakov that changed my life for the better, and I feel very lucky that I still have time to gain from the many gifts my school has to offer.

Hadassah Sunitsky, 11th

Worth It

Imagine a small blue pebble among fields of blue pebbles. As time goes by, the pebbles get blown, thrown, or kicked away; and before you it the small pebble is in a completely new place. As the blue pebble moves slowly with the wind a loud whipping sound is heard, and remarkably, all the original blue pebbles are moving with the small pebble alongside the wind.
What is high school?
As much as I’d like to say it’s getting up at seven to spend eight hours taking notes, tests and quizzes, I can’t. Because in Bnos Yaakov, I’ve learnt that it’s so much more. It’s the opportunity to let ourselves grow and discover who we are, and within that process we get to make extraordinary friends who’ve become like family, and have our backs throughout the entire journey. I used to believe that acing every subject and getting perfect scores is what mattered most, but Boruch Hashem I’m coming to understand that doing well academically is only a part of the big picture. It’s about being able to look back at the four year experience and seeing the growth and progress we’ve been strong enough to build. It’s to look back and remember the inspiring lessons taught by teachers that gave us one million percent.
Lastly and most importantly, it’s having and keeping the incredible friendships that we’ve been fortunate enough to make. Like the small blue pebble, no matter how far each of our lives takes us, we will always hold on to the love and warmth that we’ve received from Bnos Yaakov, and it will travel alongside us forever.

Racheli Bokchin, 10th
